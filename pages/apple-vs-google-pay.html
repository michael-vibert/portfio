Basics of Google Pay Vs Apple Pay Protocols

Sept. 5th 2022
image supplied https://twitter.com/alexxubyte/status/1572614943811440642

Apple Pay and Google Pay have become super popular in recent years. They are both very good protocols but they are implemented a little differently. Since these protocols go on invisibly it can be easy to forget that somehow, your phone is connecting to your bank and the payment point and deciding if you have any money, then approving or dis-approving the transaction.

In steps 1-3 in both protocols the bank card details are registered and stored for use. The Apple flow doesn't store any card information, it gives the info to the bank and the bank issues a token called a Device Account Number (DAN). Your iPhone stores the DAN in a special hardware chip in the device. The Google device takes your card details and stores them in a server. Google returns a payment token to the device. These steps take you up to step 4 on the above image.

In step five the customer selects to pay the E-Commerce server. The Apple device passes the DAN to the bank through the payment terminal. The Google pay device passes the payment token onto the Google server who then passes it on the bank.

In this diagram the red arrows represent times when the card info is available on a public network.

As you can see, the Apple Pay flow only exposes the users card info to the public network once. Which could be viewed as superior to the Google Pay flow which exposes the details with every payment.

Please feel free to improve the code, make suggestions or tell me its not doing what I think its doing, that's how we learn :)
